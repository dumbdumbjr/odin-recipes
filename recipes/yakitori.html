Yakitori

Description

Yakitori is a Japanese dish of grilled chicken skewers, seasoned with a soy-based sauce. These succulent skewers
are often enjoyed as street food or in casual parlors, where they're cooked over a charcoal fire
until perfectly charred and flavorful. The simplicty of yakitori lies in its focus on quality ingredients and expert
grilling technique.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs: Cut into small pieces and skewered.
  • Salt: Used for seasoning.
  • Soy sauce: Key component of the marinade.
  • Sake: Another ingredient in the marinade.
  • Sugar: Balances flavors and adds sweetness.
  • Green onions: Often skewered alongside chicken.
  • Garlic and ginger: Flavor enhancers.
  • Honey, rice vinegar, and sesame oil: Used in some variations of the sauce.
  • Dried red chili peppers

Steps

    • Burdock root: Scrub the skin well or peel it. Cut into thin matchsticks and soak in water for about 10 minutes to remove any bitterness.
    • Carrot: Peel and cut into thin matchsticks.
    • Dried red chili pepper (optional): Cut into small pieces

  2. Stir-Fry:
    • Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Add the burdock root and stir-fry for a few minutes until it starts to soften.
    • Add the carrot and continue to stir-fry until both vegetables are tender but still slightly crunchy.

  3. Season:
    • Add sugar, sake, mirin, and soy sauce to the skillet
    • Stir well to coat the vegetables with the sauce.
    • Simmer for a few minutes until the liquid is mostly evaporated and the vegetables are well-coated with the sauce.

  4. Finish:
    • Sprinkle with sesame seeds and stir to combine.
    • Serve hot or at room temperature.
