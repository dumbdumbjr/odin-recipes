Tempura

Description

Tempura is a Japanese dish where seafood or vegetables are lightly battered and deep-fried to create a crispy
coating. Itâ€™s often served with a dipping sauce called tentsuyu. The batter is made from flour, egg, and ice water,
resulting in a light and airy texture.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp: 1 pound
  • Flour: 1 cup
  • Cornstarch 1/4 cup
  • Ice water: 1 cup
  • Vegetable oil: For deep frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Burdock root: Scrub the skin well or peel it. Cut into thin matchsticks and soak in water for about 10 minutes to remove any bitterness.
    • Carrot: Peel and cut into thin matchsticks.
    • Dried red chili pepper (optional): Cut into small pieces
  2. Stir-Fry:
    • Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Add the burdock root and stir-fry for a few minutes until it starts to soften.
    • Add the carrot and continue to stir-fry until both vegetables are tender but still slightly crunchy.
  3. Season:
    • Add sugar, sake, mirin, and soy sauce to the skillet
    • Stir well to coat the vegetables with the sauce.
    • Simmer for a few minutes until the liquid is mostly evaporated and the vegetables are well-coated with the sauce.
  4. Finish:
    • Sprinkle with sesame seeds and stir to combine.
    • Serve hot or at room temperature.
