Tempura

Description

Tempura is a Japanese dish where seafood or vegetables are lightly battered and deep-fried to create a crispy
coating. It’s often served with a dipping sauce called tentsuyu. The batter is made from flour, egg, and ice water,
resulting in a light and airy texture.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp: 1 pound
  • Flour: 1 cup
  • Cornstarch 1/4 cup
  • Ice water: 1 cup
  • Vegetable oil: For deep frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the Shrimp:
    • Peel and devein the shrimp, leaving the tails intact.
    • Pat them dry with paper towels.

  2. Make the Tempura Batter:
    • In a bowl, mix:
      • 1 cup of flour
      • 1 cold large egg (no shell)
      • 200 ml iced water
    • Stir until just combined. The batter should be lumpy and cold.

  3. Heat the Oil:
    • Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F

  4. Coat and Fry:
    • Dip each shrimp into the batter, ensuring it's well-coated.
    • Carefully place the shrimp into the hot oil.
    • Turn once during frying.
    • Cook until the tempura is pale golden brown.

  5. Drain and Serve:
    • Remove the shrimp from the oil and let them drain on a wire rack.
    • Serve hot with a dipping sauce (soy sauce, mirin, and grated daikon radish).
